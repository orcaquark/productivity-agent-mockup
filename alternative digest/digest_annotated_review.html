The unified daily digest

One of three directions explored for the "For You" home screen — alongside a conversational message log and a swipeable card deck. This one drops the card-and-thread layout entirely: the briefing, nudge, trend, missed tasks, and kudos become a single continuous read, styled closer to a personal note than a dashboard of widgets. Items tagged OPEN are known gaps worth a ticket; items tagged UNCHANGED are carried over from the card version as-is because nothing about this alternative required touching them.

Scope note: only the "For You" home screen changes here. The personalization onboarding and settings screens reviewed alongside the card version are unaffected and aren't re-annotated in this document.

Gained

  • Reads like something written to you, not a panel of widgets scanned in any order
  • One link rule everywhere — mono caret reveals, underline commits — instead of three separate expand patterns
  • The card version's contradiction (a big 82% score sitting above a line that says "no single score") is gone
  • Full task names wrap instead of truncating inside a fixed-width chip

Traded away

  • Can't skim all five things at a glance anymore — it's a top-to-bottom read, not a scannable feed
  • Insight-vs-actionable text is a subtler visual cue than two distinct chip colors and icons
  • No progress marker if you're interrupted partway through
  • Inline text links are a smaller tap target than the old full-width buttons

What changed, and why
1
One read, not five things to skim
The card-thread version let you scan everything at once and act in whatever order you liked. This trades that for a single top-to-bottom read — closer to something someone wrote you, at the direct cost of at-a-glance skimmability. That trade-off was named going in, not discovered after the fact.
2
No .card, no .node-dot — spacing and type do the separating
Hairline dividers only show up where a heavier beat genuinely needs one (above "What got missed," above the closing kudos line), reusing the exact rule color and border-top pattern the card version's trend note already used internally, rather than inventing a new chrome language for this screen.
3
The "no single score" claim is now actually true
The card version showed a large 82% figure directly above a line stating "no single score here" — a real contradiction between the copy and the UI it sat in. This version drops the number entirely, and the trend is now the original card's own bar chart — seven day-bars, then four week-bars behind "See full month" — unboxed and dropped straight into the reading flow instead of sitting in a card. An earlier pass of this alternative used a tiny decorative sparkline here instead; it's the real chart now, just without the score and the card frame around it. (The month view gets a small white backing inside the disclosure panel, since its light bar fill nearly disappeared against the panel's violet tint otherwise.)
4
Full task names wrap instead of truncating
The card version cut long task names off with an ellipsis inside a fixed-width row. Free-flowing paragraph text just wraps to a second line, so nothing about a task's name is ever hidden.
Two link treatments, one rule
5
Mono + caret reveals, underline commits
One consistent primitive runs through the whole screen: "Why am I seeing this" and "See full month" are JetBrains Mono with a rotating caret and change nothing when tapped. "Move it," "Not now," "Send kudos," "Skip," both missed-task actions, and the Gym ring link are Inter with an underline and actually do something. That's one visual rule standing in for three separate expand patterns (chip, why-link, trend-toggle) in the card version.
6
The insight/action distinction survives without chips
"Mornings clearly work for you" is now italic body text — a read-only observation. The still-open Gym ring is a sage-colored underlined link — something to act on — and opens the same ring-deep-link explainer modal as the card version, unchanged. This is a real trade-off, not a clean win: it's more subtle than the old two-color, two-icon chip system, and there's no caption spelling out the difference the way "Purple = a pattern · Green = an open ring" did in the card version. Worth watching in testing for whether this reads as decorative rather than actionable.
Resolving in place
7
Every action rewrites its own sentence, not a separate row
The card version showed a full-width feedback row beneath its buttons after resolving. Here, "Move it" / "Not now" become "Moved to 8:40am ✓ — Undo" inline in the exact spot the original text sat. Same pattern for both missed items and for kudos. Undo is preserved everywhere it existed before — nothing lost reversibility in the rewrite.
8
The nudge quote is the one place that still looks like a "card"
A violet rule down the left edge, not a bordered box — but it's deliberately the one element that interrupts the digest's reading rhythm, so a real decision (moving a task's time) doesn't slide past the way a passive sentence could. Everything else flows; this alone stands apart on purpose.
Cold start & honesty
9
Cold start is one short, unstyled paragraph
No leftover card frame around the empty state — just a sentence explaining what's missing and when it unlocks, matching the "short unstyled paragraph" instruction this alternative was built to.
10
"Add friends" is back — resolved, not just reworded
The card version's review flagged "Add friends" in the New user state as a button not wired to anything. An earlier pass of this alternative dropped it rather than reproduce that dead affordance. It's back now with a real interaction: tapping it opens the same placeholder-modal pattern already used for the still-open Gym ring, generalized to cover more than one destination (icon, title, and body swap based on which link was tapped). The modal states plainly that adding a friend involves a request and their acceptance — more than a single tap — so it shows the intended destination (the Friends tab) instead of faking that flow.
Carried over unchanged
11
State switcher and navbarUNCHANGED
The preview-only Active/New user toggle and the five-tab navbar are identical to the card version, down to the accessibility handling. Neither was in scope for a home-screen layout or modal change, so neither was touched. (The ring modal itself doesn't get this tag anymore — see note 10; it's now a shared component covering two destinations instead of one.)
12
Same underlying data, same copy voiceUNCHANGED
Every sentence in this digest traces back to the same five data points the card version used — rings closed, the nudge's reasoning, the week's shape, the two missed tasks, Jordan's streak. This is a different presentation of the same personalization, not a new feature or a new tone.
Open
13
Task names are still fully readable on a shared or unlocked screenOPEN
Carried over unresolved from the card review — and arguably sharper here, since "Submit expense report" now sits in plain paragraph text with no card boundary that a future "blur sensitive text" setting could target independently of the sentence around it.
14
Jordan's streak still surfaces without Jordan's consentOPEN
Unchanged from the card version's open item. The closing line still names a friend's activity to someone else with no opt-in on the friend's side — this alternative's presentation doesn't touch the underlying consent gap.
15
Inline text links are a smaller, harder-to-aim target than full-width buttonsOPEN
"Move it" and "Not now" used to be 42px-tall buttons; as inline text they're only as tall as their line-height. Worth a real touch-target pass before this ships, especially "Skip" and "Undo," which land close together in the closing line.
16
No indication of how far into the digest you've readOPEN
A feed lets you glance at all five items at once and see what's left. A single scroll gives no such cue — someone interrupted mid-read has nothing telling them where they left off tomorrow.

Considered, not yet designed

Real problems worth a ticket, but solving them changes scope beyond this one alternative — flagging so they don't get lost between this review and a spec.

  • A "read so far" marker or pagination for longer digests
  • How this scales past the fixed five beats — a day with two nudges or four missed tasks doesn't have a defined layout yet
  • A "blur sensitive text" setting, given note 13 above
  • Friend-side consent for surfacing streaks (same open item as the card version)
  • RTL mirroring of the nudge blockquote's left-side violet rule
  • Day/week labels (M T W T F S · Wk 1–4) may not abbreviate meaningfully in every locale
  • Loading and network-error states
  • A "sent" timestamp, to make the "digest, not a feed" framing literal
  • Formal touch-target audit for every inline action link (relates to note 15)

Guardrails these decisions map back to

The recurring principles behind the choices above — the same ones the card version was checked against, with one addition specific to a chrome-free layout.

Tone & framing

  • Describe, don't judge — patterns and reasons stay in neutral prose, never a verdict on the person
  • No single score, for real this time — the number is gone, not just downplayed next to text that says it isn't there

Reversibility

  • Undo where it's cheap — nudge, missed-task, and kudos actions, same as before
  • One beat still interrupts the flow on purpose — the nudge is the single place a real decision doesn't just slide past as body copy

Honesty about state

  • Cold start is explained, not blank — a real sentence, no invented pattern
  • No dead links — "Add friends" got a real interaction (the shared placeholder modal) instead of staying unwired

One consistent primitive

  • Two link types, used everywhere — mono caret to reveal, underline to commit — instead of a different expand pattern per card
  • Spend the one visual accent deliberately — the violet rule on the nudge is the screen's single interruption; everything else stays quiet
